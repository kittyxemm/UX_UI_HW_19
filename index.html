Case Studies

Modernizing Indigenous Services Canada to Reflect a Changing Culture

In November 2022, I was tasked with producing a potential design overhaul of a government department webpage. Due to its inconsistent page elements, excessive information, lack of clear nagivational structure, and images that did not seem culturally informed, I chose Indigenous Services Canada.

Indigenous people represented approximately five percent of the total population of Canada in the 2021 census. As the first residents of Canada, I felt this group deserved proper representation that reflected change and empowerment.

I achieved this by using colours such as orange, which is the colour used in many campaigns to promote Indigenous rights, as well as photography from Pexels and Unsplash that featured Indigenous people and cultural artifacts.

Journey to Diversity Workplaces: Redesigning the User Journey

Journey to Diversity Workplaces, or J2DW, is a non-profit organization that aims to raise awareness about the needs of diverse individuals in the workplace.

In December 2022, my team took on the task of redesigning the Journey to Diversity Workplaces website interface. We felt strongly that a better-organized navigation, simplified page organization, and relevant information would greatly assist the design and the users visiting the website.

In order to achieve this, we were fortunate enough to host a stakeholder interview with CEO Peter Tretter and gather some information about the organization that may help describe their mission and encourage users to donate to their causes. The information we learned during this interview would then begin to inform the rest of our design process.
